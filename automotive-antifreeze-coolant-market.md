<p><p>The automotive antifreeze and coolant market is primarily categorized into four types: ethylene glycol, propylene glycol, glycerin, and others. Ethylene glycol is the most common, known for its effective heat transfer and freeze protection properties. Propylene glycol is less toxic and often preferred for vehicles used in food transport. Glycerin is a biodegradable alternative, while "others" includes various formulations that address specific requirements such as high-performance and environmentally-friendly applications. Each type serves unique thermal management needs in vehicles.</p></p>
