<p><p>The Hydrogenated Pygas market is divided into two main types based on the hydrocracking process used. The Single-Stage Hydrocracking Process involves a single reactor where pyrolysis gasoline (pygas) is treated with hydrogen to enhance its quality and yield, resulting in a straightforward, cost-effective method. In contrast, the Two-Stage Hydrocracking Process employs multiple reactors, allowing for more refined control over the reaction conditions, leading to higher purity products but at increased operational complexity and cost.</p></p>
