<p><p>The wire and cable compounds market is primarily divided into two types: halogenated and non-halogenated polymers. Halogenated polymers, containing elements like chlorine or bromine, are known for their flame-retardant properties, providing enhanced safety in electrical applications. Non-halogenated polymers, on the other hand, are more environmentally friendly and pose less health risk during combustion. They often offer sufficient thermal and electrical performance while meeting regulatory standards for sustainability and safety, making them increasingly popular in various applications.</p></p>
