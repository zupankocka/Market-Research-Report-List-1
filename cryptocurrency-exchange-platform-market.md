<p><p>Cryptocurrency exchange platforms are categorized into centralized and decentralized exchanges. Centralized exchanges (CEX) act as intermediaries, facilitating trades by matching buy and sell orders, offering high liquidity, and user-friendly interfaces. They often require user accounts and hold funds, enhancing security but risking potential hacks. Conversely, decentralized exchanges (DEX) operate on a peer-to-peer network without intermediaries, allowing users to retain control of their funds, promoting privacy and security. However, DEXs may have lower liquidity and more complex interfaces, appealing to experienced traders.</p></p>
