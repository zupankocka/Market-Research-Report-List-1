<p><p>The chemical imaging systems market comprises several key types, each serving distinct analytical needs. Spectrochemical imaging microscopes utilize spectroscopy for chemical composition analysis. Confocal Raman microscopes provide high-resolution imaging based on inelastic scattering of light. Atomic force microscopes achieve nanoscale resolution by measuring surface forces. Dispersive microscopes separate light into its component wavelengths to analyze materials. Other techniques may include various hybrid systems or novel imaging technologies, broadening the scope of material characterization and analysis in research and industrial applications.</p></p>
