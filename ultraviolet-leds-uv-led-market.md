<p><p>Ultraviolet LEDs (UV-LEDs) are categorized into three main types based on their emission wavelengths. UV-A LEDs operate in the 315-400 nm range, primarily used for curing, biotechnology, and pest control. UV-B LEDs, emitting between 280-315 nm, are often utilized in therapeutic applications and agricultural settings. UV-C LEDs, with wavelengths between 200-280 nm, are effective for disinfection and sterilization due to their germicidal properties. Other market categories may include specialized applications or hybrid UV technologies.</p></p>
